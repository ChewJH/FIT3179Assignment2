Tourism Industry in Malaysia

International Tourists in Malaysia

Malaysia is a highly popular travel destination ranked 27th among 50 Best Places to Travel in the World. The capital city, Kuala Lumpur is the most commonly visited city in Malaysia. In 2022, the tourism industry contributed RM10.1 million towards the country's GDP

International Tourists by Country

Malaysia has managed to attract 134,728 tourists in 2021. Prior to the COVID 19 pandemic, the country had an average 2.3 million visitors each year. Most tourists come from neighbouring countries especially within the South-East Asia region.

Top 10 Countries with Highest Number of Tourists

  • The top 3 countries with the most inbound tourists are Singapore, Indonesia & China.
    • Singaporeans and Indonesians could be attracted to Malaysia due to its proximity and similar cultures.
    • Chinese on the other hand may be attracted to the country due to the large local Chinese population and similar weather conditions.
  • Only 2 out of the 10 countries on the chart lie outside of Asia.

Tourist Expenditure by Category

The largest proportion of expenses by tourists is spent on country specific tourism goods at 33%. These can include items such as souveniers and other cultural goods

Tourism Industry's Contribution towards Malaysia's Economy

GDP Contribution of Tourism Industry

The tourism industry, which is part of the services category, has contributed an average of RM800 million to the country's GDP or about 55% of its GDP

Employment in the Tourism Industry

The percentage of people employed in the tourism industry is 6.7% on average of the total workforce. The manufacturing and wholesale and retail trade sectors have the highest percentage of workforce

Wages of Tourism Industry

The average median salary of within in the tourism industry is RM1170 monthly, lower than the national average median monthly salary of RM2144. In contrast, the highest paying industry is the education industry has an average median monthly salary of RM4170.
